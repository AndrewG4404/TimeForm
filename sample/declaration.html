Declaration of Independence

The Declaration of Independence was adopted by the Continental Congress on July 4, 1776. It announced that the thirteen American colonies were at war with the Kingdom of Great Britain, and thus no longer considered themselves part of the British Empire. Drafted by Thomas Jefferson, the document asserted the colonies' right to self-govern and provided a detailed justification for the decision to become independent.

Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
Date: July 4, 1776
Historical Figures: Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin
